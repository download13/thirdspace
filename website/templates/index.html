Third Space: Space For All

What is a Third Space?

For many people, the First Place is the home. The Second Place might be the office or school. The Third Place is the place in-between. The church, the barber shop, the library, the local cafe are familiar examples of such places.

Who are we?

We are citizens concerned with the unavailability of Third Spaces that fit the description above: local, cheap or free, highly-available, and inclusive.

We believe that the Third Space is not only for urban, well-resourced people, but also for the suburbs and rural areas, for uncool and awkward, for young and old -- for all.

What are we doing?

Our idea, originally conceived at the 2015 CodeAcross event, is to provide a platform for communities, civic groups, governments, and landlords to collaborate in establishing Third Spaces in communities where there is both a need and a desire. We connect those with the vision and the leadership ability with those who can make their vision a reality.

How can you help?

Currently, we are working on three main projects:

  • Developing a business and operations plan for Third Space. We are researching similar projects and groups to see what does and doesn't work.
  • Considering ideas for what kind of technological platform would serve the goals of our mission.
  • Identifying groups interested in establishing Third Spaces, potential sites for Third Spaces, and legal and real estate professionals who can navigate the complexities leasing and developing Third Spaces.

If you live in or near Sacramento, please join us on Second Saturdays at the Urban Hive. If you have any questions, please feel free to contact us.
